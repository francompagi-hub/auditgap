The Assessment

What the audit covers

Our WCAG 2.1 AA gap analysis combines automated scanning with structured manual review across five critical areas. Every finding is verified, documented, and delivered with remediation guidance.

  • Automated WCAG 2.1 AA scanning — all pages

    Full-site scan using industry-standard accessibility testing tools. Every page assessed against WCAG 2.1 Level AA criteria. Not just the homepage.

  • Manual keyboard navigation testing

    Automated scans miss barriers that require human interaction to surface. Manual testing verifies that all interactive elements — menus, dropdowns, cart, checkout — are reachable and operable without a mouse.

  • Screen reader compatibility review

    ARIA labels, alt text, heading structure, and focus management. Identifies the specific barriers that prevent screen reader users from completing a purchase.

  • Colour contrast and visual accessibility

    All foreground/background combinations measured against the WCAG 4.5:1 contrast ratio requirement. Typography size, weight, and readability evaluated.

  • Form, checkout, and cart accessibility audit

    The highest-risk area for e-commerce stores. Error messaging, label associations, submit button accessibility, and payment form compliance all assessed.

The Methodology

How the audit works

Every audit is conducted against WCAG 2.1 AA — the standard courts reference in ADA website litigation. This is not an internal standard or proprietary framework. It is the publicly documented benchmark that governs accessibility compliance determinations in US federal court.

The audit is conducted on your public-facing website. No CMS credentials, admin access, or analytics access is required. We assess what plaintiff firms assess: your publicly visible site.

Every finding is verified manually before documentation. Automated scanning tools identify approximately 60–70% of real accessibility barriers. The remainder — keyboard navigation failures, focus management issues, screen reader incompatibilities — require human testing to surface. Both methods are applied on every audit.

The output is designed for immediate use by your developer without additional interpretation. Audit Gap does not sell remediation services, subscriptions, or retainers. The engagement is a report. The report is the product.

What a finding looks like

Issue Product images missing descriptive alt text
WCAG Criterion 1.1.1 — Non-text Content (Level A)
Severity Critical
Impact Screen reader users cannot identify products during browsing or checkout. This is a direct barrier to purchase completion for visually impaired users — the exact scenario ADA litigation targets.
Fix Add descriptive alt attributes to all product images. Alt text should describe the product specifically, not use generic labels like "image1" or "product photo."
